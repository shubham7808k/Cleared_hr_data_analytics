♦️ Overview
This project leverages IBM Cognos Analytics to transform raw data into actionable business intelligence. By utilizing advanced reporting and interactive dashboards, the project identifies key trends, optimizes data exploration, and allows the HR Department to take the appropriate steps to keep the organization running smoothly and profitably.

📝 Objectives
Data Modeling: Creating a robust data module to ensure accurate reporting across divisions and job titles.

Interactive Visualization: Designing intuitive pie and bar charts for easy exploration of workforce distribution.

Business Insights: Extracting high-impact findings regarding employment types and departmental diversity.

Technical Mastery: Demonstrating proficiency in IBM Cognos tools, including Count Distinct aggregations, filters, and cross-functional data mapping.

📂 Dataset Profile
Name: HR Analytics Data

Source: Kaggle (HR Analytics Dataset)

Format: CSV

Dimensions: 2846 rows × 28 columns

Domain: Human Resource Management

Description: This dataset contains detailed information about employees, departments, and job titles. This project interprets organizational data to find people-related trends and operational insights.

📊 Dashboard Features
KPIs: Total Employee Count (2,845), Total Divisions (26), and Distinct Job Titles.

State by Division: A comprehensive pie chart breakdown showing headcount across functional areas like Field Operations and Technology/IT.

Division by Title: A bar chart analyzing the "Division Count" per job title to identify cross-departmental roles.

Employee Type Distribution: A clear breakdown of Full-Time, Contract, and Part-Time staff ratios.

📍 Key Insights
Trend and Categorical Analysis:

High Impact Divisions: The Field Operations and General - Con divisions represent the largest workforce segments with 21 and 20 units respectively.

Cross-Functional Roles: Data Analysts show the highest level of departmental integration, appearing across 16 different divisions, followed by Accountants in 13 divisions.

Workforce Flexibility: The organization maintains a balanced labor force with 997 Full-Time employees, followed closely by 951 Contract workers and 897 Part-Time staff.

Anomaly Detection:

Operational Concentration: Certain divisions, such as Safety and ISP, have significantly lower headcounts (1-2), suggesting specialized or lean operational units compared to high-volume divisions like Field Operations.

Role Specialization: The Enterprise Architect role is highly specialized, appearing in only 2 divisions, indicating a very focused or centralized reporting structure.

Prediction:

Based on the current growth of Contract vs. Full-Time counts (separated by only 46 units), it is projected that the contract workforce may equal the full-time headcount by Q3 of next year if the current hiring trend continues.

♦️ Conclusion
This project demonstrates the transition from raw data to business value. Through the use of IBM Cognos, I have created a scalable reporting framework that allows users to not only see the company's key employee details but also understand their distribution patterns, identify cross-functional talent, and help make informed decisions to optimize the organizational workflow.
